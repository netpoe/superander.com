Detailed information

about how to buy and invest in SANDER1 tokens

Some of these concepts may sound new to you, but the main idea is that you will hold "shares" of the 1st studio album of Súper Ánder and that you will receive dividends in form of ETH every 3 months depending on the percentage of shares that you posess.

In order to hold or have control of these "shares", which we'll call tokens, you need an Ethereum wallet. A wallet is an application that can either be a mobile app, a browser extension, a web app or even hardware.

Any of these wallets will allow you to access the same exact Ethereum address via your private keys.

In this tutorial, we will display screenshots of Trust Wallet, a popular mobile application for iOS or Android that allows you to create and manage Ethereum addresses.

Creating an Ethereum wallet

Once you've downloaded Trust Wallet, go to the settings tab and click on "Wallets".

The following view will display a list of all your wallets.

Think of each wallet as a single Ethereum address.

Click on the plus (+) sign at the top-right part of the screen.

Swipe through the steps and click on "Create wallet".

The 12 words seed phrase

Also known as the "mnemonic phrase" or "mnemonic seed", this 12 words phrase is unique to the entire Ethereum network.

THIS IS YOUR PASSWORD TO YOUR WALLET. It is not stored in any server or database, only in your device. Losing your phrase means losing your funds, so pay attention.

At this point, Trust Wallet should request that you copy and store these 12 words somewhere safe. Copy and paste the phrase in your password manager or in a place only you have access to and confirm the phrase in the next view.

Managing your wallet

Your new wallet should be listed in the Wallets index.

You can rename your wallet to whatever tickles your fancy.

By clicking on any of your wallets, you'll see your ETH balance and all of the tokens and coins linked to your wallet.

A new wallet has none of the above.

Buying tokens

Clicking on the Ethereum balance should display only your ETH balance and the options to SEND and RECEIVE ETH.

It is time to buy SANDER1 tokens.

Click on the SEND button.

Next, we will input the recipient address. You can either copy and paste this address: {{ ens.crowdsale.address }} or scan this QR code by clicking in the QR icon at the right of the input.

Input the amount of ETH you are willing to invest and click send.

This will start an Ethereum transaction that you can track by clicking on the transaction item that will be generated after sending the ETH to the contract address.

About the SuperCrowdsale smart contract

The Ethereum address that you will input in the recipient address is not the wallet.superander.eth address. This other address is special because you will send the ETH funds to a smart contract, not to another wallet.

This smart contract is a custom crowdsale contract called SuperCrowdsale, (you can check the code here). This smart contract is specifically designed to send SANDER1 tokens in exchange of ETH.

When the SuperCrowdsale contract receives an ETH transaction with a value greater than 0, it will calculate the amount of tokens that correspond to the value of ETH sent and it will transfer you the tokens and transfer the ETH to the wallet.superander.eth address. After this, you should see your SANDER1 tokens listed in your wallet.

The SuperCrowdsale contract is a capped crowdsale contract, meaning that once it reaches its ETH cap ({{ config.crowdsale.cap }} ETH), it will block future transactions.
